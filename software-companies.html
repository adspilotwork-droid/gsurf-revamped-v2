The Problem

What breaks with generic broadband.

Most ISPs sell the same residential-grade line to everyone and hope it works. For software teams, it doesn't — here's what we hear from teams switching to GSurf.

Asymmetric pipes choke on upload

Most consumer broadband gives you a fat download and a starved upload. Modern dev work is upload-heavy — Git pushes, container registries, backup syncs. A 100/20 split kills your team the moment two devs push simultaneously.

Dynamic IPs break every VPN

Your IP changes, firewall allowlists drop, bastion hosts disappear, CI/CD fails to connect back. Static IP isn't a luxury for software teams — it's the baseline.

Peak-hour contention kills Zoom

Residential lines are oversubscribed 1:20 or worse. At 10am every Monday your all-hands turns into slideshow mode and nobody can hear the CTO.

Common questions

Quick answers before you book.

The questions we hear most from prospects in this segment. If yours isn't here, our sales team answers on a ten-minute call.

Q1

Is the bandwidth truly symmetric?

Yes — every GSurf enterprise plan is symmetric by default. A 1 Gbps plan delivers 1 Gbps upload and 1 Gbps download, contract-enforced. Upload speeds for Git, containers and cloud sync match download speeds.

Q2

Do I get a static IP address?

Yes. Every enterprise plan includes one static IPv4 address at no additional cost. Additional IPs, /29 and /28 allocations are available on request. BGP announcement of your own IP space is supported.

Q3

What is the latency to AWS Mumbai?

Our peering configuration delivers sub-5ms round-trip latency to AWS ap-south-1 (Mumbai), Azure Mumbai, and GCP Mumbai regions from our Whitefield plant.

Q4

Can I upgrade from 300 Mbps to 1 Gbps later?

Yes, upgrades happen in our provisioning console without a new install, new ONT, or downtime. Email sales, the plan profile is bumped, and the change typically lands before your next stand-up.
