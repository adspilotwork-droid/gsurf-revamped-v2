The Problem

What breaks with generic broadband.

Most ISPs sell the same residential-grade line to everyone and hope it works. For hospitals and clinics, it doesn't — here's what we hear from teams switching to GSurf.

PACS imaging transfers are enormous

A single CT study can be 500MB. Your imaging team waits three minutes per upload to cloud archive or for teleradiology. Every drop shows up as a backed-up queue by end of shift.

A dropped connection halts EMR

When HIMS goes offline, front-desk billing stops, prescriptions can't be written digitally, discharge summaries don't generate. A 15-minute outage becomes 45 minutes of recovery.

Telemedicine quality is non-negotiable

Patients don't tolerate jittery video with a doctor. Neither do specialists reviewing a remote consult. Residential broadband cannot sustain consulting-grade video.

The Solution

Every plan tuned to how you actually work.

These aren't generic features — each one is a direct response to a pain point our customers keep raising. We're a local operator. We listen, and we ship.

Dual-path fiber redundancy

Two physically diverse fiber paths into your building. If one is cut during roadwork, traffic auto-routes to the second. Available on request for critical-care institutions.

Low-latency peering for imaging

Optimised routes to major Indian medical cloud providers and teleradiology platforms. Image transfers complete in seconds, not minutes.

HIPAA-ready segmentation

Strict VLAN and ACL separation between clinical devices, administrative systems, guest Wi-Fi, and CCTV. Documented configuration delivered alongside commissioning.

4G/5G failover for critical uptime

For OPD, emergency and ICU-adjacent systems — instant failover keeps HIMS and EMR accessible. Measured in seconds of switchover, not minutes.

Priority support · 2-hour response

Healthcare customers get our fastest SLA tier. Named NOC engineer, direct phone line that bypasses queue, escalation to engineering head within the first hour.

Monthly network health reports

Delivered to your IT head or COO — showing uptime, incidents, MTTR and bandwidth trends. No surprises on renewal.
