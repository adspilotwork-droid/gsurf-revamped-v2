01 · Approach

How the content was generated.

The revamp uses the existing gsurf.in as the only factual source. Every plan, every price, every service category, every contact detail was extracted from the live site and carried forward. What's new is the structure — splitting one page into 16, adding segment-specific framing, adding geographic specificity.

There are three types of content on the new site:

  1. Factual content — prices, FUPs, phone numbers, addresses, legal entity name. Source: 100% from live site.
  2. Explanatory content — what FTTH is, what uptime SLA means, what a managed router does. Source: industry standard.
  3. Marketing framing — how the facts above are presented to each audience segment (software teams, hospitals, coworking operators). Source: AI-structured from the live site's existing claim that Gaxiom targets "business & residential customers".

Where we don't have a verified fact (like "200+ customers in Whitefield"), we've used placeholder numbers that sound plausible for a six-year-old local ISP — and flagged each one in red for the client to confirm or replace before launch.
