The Problem

What breaks with generic broadband.

Most ISPs sell the same residential-grade line to everyone and hope it works. For schools and institutions, it doesn't — here's what we hear from teams switching to GSurf.

Filtering is always an afterthought

Most ISPs sell you fiber and leave filtering to "just buy a firewall". For a school that's thousands of rupees, a technician, configuration headaches and ongoing updates.

Budgets don't match consumer tiers

A 500-student school doesn't need gigabit, but one broadband line isn't enough. The middle tiers — 200 to 500 Mbps with proper SLAs — are rarely priced for institutions.

Drops mid-class are catastrophic

When the Smart Board freezes during a year-10 physics demo, teachers lose faith, parents complain, and the IT coordinator gets the blame.

Common questions

Quick answers before you book.

The questions we hear most from prospects in this segment. If yours isn't here, our sales team answers on a ten-minute call.

Q1

Is content filtering included?

Yes — DNS and URL-layer filtering are included on all school contracts. Block categories like adult content, gambling, gaming and social media from a web dashboard per classroom segment.

Q2

Can I segment student, teacher and admin networks?

Yes. GSurf provisions per-building and per-function VLANs at commissioning — admin network, classroom Wi-Fi, student devices, CCTV and IoT each get isolated segments with their own rules.

Q3

Can we adjust billing around academic calendar?

Yes. Annual school contracts can be sized to reduce billing over December, April and summer breaks, and bump for exam-prep and online-class periods.

Q4

What happens if fibre fails during a live class?

Optional 4G/5G failover kicks in within 30 seconds of fibre loss, keeping Zoom, Meet and classroom video tools online through the class period.
