The Problem

What breaks with generic broadband.

Most ISPs sell the same residential-grade line to everyone and hope it works. For small and mid businesses, it doesn't — here's what we hear from teams switching to GSurf.

Business pricing is cliff-edged

The jump from home plans to enterprise is a cliff. A 20-person CA firm doesn't need a ₹40k/month leased line, but can't run on a ₹900 home plan. The middle needs to exist.

One connection is single point of failure

When fiber drops, the office stops. Cash registers freeze, Tally goes offline, emails queue. You need failover without buying a second fiber.

Managed services are always extra

Firewall? Extra ₹8,000/month. Managed router? ₹3,000. VoIP integration? ₹2,500 setup. The "affordable" plan isn't affordable once you add essentials.

What is the minimum plan for a small office?

100 Mbps business at ₹1,800/month is our most popular tier for 10–30 person offices. It handles cloud accounting software, video calls, CCTV upload and typical office work with room to grow.

Yes — a dual-band Wi-Fi 6 router comes pre-configured for your office and is remotely managed by GSurf. Firmware updates, reboots and config backups happen without your involvement.
